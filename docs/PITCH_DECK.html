The problem

Getting the first antibiotic right is life or death

A patient is admitted with a Klebsiella infection. The doctor must choose an antibiotic now — but culture-based susceptibility testing takes 24–72 hours.

That gap — 77% vs 9% — is the entire problem. It is exactly the decision Genome Firewall accelerates, from days to seconds.

Why existing tools aren't enough

Rule-based tools give a confident binary call

  • No calibrated confidence — a yes/no hides how sure the tool is (published balanced accuracy 0.52–0.66).
  • No abstention — they answer even on resistance mechanisms they've never characterized, exactly when a confident wrong answer is most dangerous.
  • In-distribution validation only — scores look good until a new lineage or a future isolate arrives.

We give a calibrated probability with an honest refusal when the genome is unfamiliar — the difference between a tool a clinician reads and one a clinician can trust.

How it works

Architecture

Genome FASTA → AMRFinderPlus markers → per-drug calibrated logistic regression → decision layer: out-of-distribution firewall + target-presence gate + stewardship. Data: BV-BRC, laboratory-measured labels only.

Results · honest generalization

Tested two ways most tools skip

Unseen lineages (homology-grouped split): balanced accuracy 0.94–0.98, vs ~0.6 for rule-based tools.

Future isolates (temporal split, train ≤2014 → test ≥2015): 0.78–0.94 despite real resistance drift.

Where it drops, the firewall abstains more instead of guessing — OOD abstentions rose from ~5 to 71–90 per drug under drift.
